Proposed workflow

From preparation to a maintained Open Research Record.

Readiness screening, scientific review, open-science review and technical assessment remain distinct. An unconflicted JORN editor makes the final publication decision.

Workflow under development. Formal submissions are not open. Roles, service levels, forms, systems, criteria and policies require approval before operation.

The 21 stages¶

01

Use the Implementation Guide

Authors assess applicable research objects, practices and evidence.

02

Complete readiness self-assessment

Gaps, restrictions, external assessments and human-verification needs are recorded without a score.

03

Deposit and version objects

The manuscript, data, software, workflows and other applicable materials receive exact versions, identifiers and access terms.

04

Receive proposal or submission

During the founding phase, JORN receives only pilot expressions of interest—not formal journal submissions.

05

Run deterministic technical checks

Approved tools resolve identifiers, validate files and links, detect metadata or licence fields, and compare checksums where appropriate.

06

Conduct AI-assisted triage

If approved and consented, an AI system analyses authorised materials for readiness and routing—not acceptance.

07

Editor confirms triage

A human confirms, changes or escalates every pilot result and records disagreement.

08

Screen scope, ethics and completeness

An editor determines whether responsible evaluation is possible and requests remediation where appropriate.

09

Select scientific-review route

The editor assesses external, coordinated or JORN internal review against the required expertise profile.

10

Use an external community where suitable

Qualified, version-specific external review may reduce duplication under published recognition criteria.

11

Recruit JORN reviewers where needed

When no suitable community is available, JORN appoints qualified scientific reviewers directly.

12

Fill expertise gaps

Supplementary reviewers address gaps in external review, methods, statistics, governance or domain coverage.

13

Review open-science implementation

Qualified reviewers verify documentation, identifiers, access, licences, versions, provenance and connections.

14

Assess artifacts where applicable

Evaluators inspect function, usability, workflows, synthetic data, secure verification or reproduction for bounded claims.

15

Authors revise exact versions

Responses and new repository versions preserve what changed and why.

16

Evaluate revisions

Reviewers or editors check responses and changed objects within their expertise and declared scope.

17

JORN editor decides

An unconflicted editor integrates all evidence and formally accepts or rejects the exact package. AI and badges do not decide.

18

Produce the Open Research Record

JORN prepares the citation, DOI metadata, object relationships, reports, decisions, statements and status.

19

Approve certification and badges

Human approvers verify each level and bounded claim against exact evidence and criteria.

20

Publish and preserve

Accessible HTML, PDF, citation exports and structured metadata are released under the JORN title.

21

Maintain stewardship

JORN manages corrections, updates, expressions of concern, withdrawal, retraction, links and certification status.

Screening outcomes are not decisions¶

“Ready for human review,” “author action required,” and “human editorial triage required” are routing outcomes. During the pilot, AI cannot autonomously reject work. Authors may correct factual errors and request human reconsideration.

Decision taxonomy¶

Decision Accountable actor Meaning
Decline before review JORN editor Outside scope or not responsibly reviewable after human screening
Major or minor revision JORN editor Specified changes are required
Accept JORN editor Exact identified version accepted for JORN publication
Reject JORN editor Publication criteria not met after accountable evaluation
Withdraw Author or journal under policy Process ended with reason and record handling defined
Pause JORN editor Ethics, integrity, access, expertise or legal question must be resolved

Public review record¶

The proposed default after acceptance is public commissioned reports, author responses and editorial decisions. Reviewers sign only by consent. External reports show their source and recognised scope. Public comments remain labelled as community feedback. Rejected or withdrawn work requires separate consent and policy.

Appeals and integrity¶

Appeals must go to an unconflicted editor or panel not responsible for the original decision. Complaints and integrity concerns require confidential intake, recusal, appropriate expertise, evidence handling and protection against retaliation.
