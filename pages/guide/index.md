## Who the guide is for

Researchers may use the guide while planning a project or preparing a paper. It is also designed for research groups, peer reviewers, librarians, research-software engineers, data stewards, funders, institutions, students, and independent researchers. Use does not require an intention to publish with JORN.

## Seven questions to answer

1. How open and connected is the research now?
2. What is missing or insufficiently documented?
3. Why does each element matter?
4. What practical action would improve it?
5. Which standards, repositories, services, or communities could help?
6. What evidence would JORN require for verification?
7. Which claims require human, disciplinary, ethical, privacy, or technical review?

## Work through the dimensions

| Area | Check | Evidence to retain |
| --- | --- | --- |
| People and organisations | Authors, ORCIDs, affiliations, ROR identifiers, CRediT roles, contacts | Identifier records and contribution statement |
| Research plan | Protocol, preregistration, governance, deviations, ethics and consent | Persistent records, approvals, amendments and explanations |
| Manuscript | Discoverable deposit, exact version, licence, methods and declarations | Identifier, version, licence and checksum where appropriate |
| Data | Access category, identifier, version, documentation, licence or terms, provenance | Repository record, data-management and access documentation |
| Software and models | Source, archived release, identifier, licence, tests, dependencies and citation | Release DOI or SWHID, commit, environment and test evidence |
| Workflows and environments | Executable steps, notebooks, containers, dependency locks and expected outputs | Versioned workflow and execution instructions |
| Figures and media | Source data, generation process, captions, attribution, alt text and provenance | Versioned source-material package and relationships |
| Review and evaluation | Scientific review, open-science review and artifact checks kept distinct | Reports, exact assessed versions, criteria and responses |
| Stewardship | Preservation, maintenance, corrections, access changes and status history | Preservation route, owner and update plan |

The complete dimensional set also covers synthetic or simulated data, models, materials, licences, persistent identifiers, citation, accessibility, community or Indigenous governance, reproduction, replication, and maintenance.

## For each dimension

Record its applicability, current [implementation level](../implementation-levels/), readiness state, evidence, gaps, next action, responsible person, dependencies, restrictions, and review needs. Do not calculate a numerical openness score.

### Assessment states

- **Self-assessed:** completed by an author or group without independent verification.
- **Evidence supplied:** links and documentation have been supplied, but JORN has not verified them.
- **AI-assisted screening:** an approved system generated a preliminary report on a public or securely authorised package. This is neither certification nor peer review.
- **JORN verified:** a qualified JORN editor, reviewer, evaluator, or recognised external assessor examined the evidence under a published criteria version.

Only **JORN verified** evidence may support official JORN certification.

## Improve responsibly

Open does not mean public at any cost. Research data should be as open as ethically, legally, and methodologically possible, and as restricted as necessary. Controlled access can meet the framework when the restriction, public metadata, governance, and responsible verification route are adequate.

JORN prescribes outcomes and evidence, not vendors. The [Services and Tools Guide](../services/) lists selection criteria and examples; equivalent services are acceptable when they meet the same requirements.
