## How to select a service

For every category, assess persistent identifiers, explicit versioning, licences or access terms, preservation, metadata export, interoperability, accessibility, privacy, sustainability, governance, cost, and discipline fit. Prefer services that make exact versions and relationships machine-readable and allow metadata export or migration.

| Need | Required outcome | Examples to investigate | Key cautions |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Manuscript or preprint | Discoverable, versioned manuscript with stable metadata | arXiv, bioRxiv, medRxiv, SciELO Preprints, OSF, institutional or disciplinary repositories | Scope, moderation, version and licence support vary |
| General research objects | Citable deposit with metadata and preservation | Zenodo, Figshare, OSF, Dataverse, institutional repositories | Costs, governance, access controls and preservation vary |
| Data | Appropriate disciplinary or general archive; controlled access where needed | Dryad, Dataverse, disciplinary and institutional archives | Do not place sensitive data in an unsuitable public service |
| Software development | Public history, collaboration and issue tracking | GitHub, GitLab, Codeberg | A development repository is not an immutable archive |
| Software preservation | Archived release and persistent identifier | Zenodo, Software Heritage | Record release, commit, DOI or SWHID and relationships |
| Software review | Version-specific external assessment | JOSS, pyOpenSci, rOpenSci | Different organisations have different scopes; review does not validate the paper |
| Workflows and execution | Preserved environment and executable instructions | Binder-compatible environments, containers, workflow archives | Hosted execution is not necessarily long-term preservation |
| Protocols and registration | Time-stamped, versioned research plan | protocols.io, OSF and discipline-specific registries | Registration and peer review are different claims |
| Identities and roles | Persistent people, organisation and contribution metadata | ORCID, ROR, CRediT | Authors must verify mappings and roles |
| Metadata and provenance | Structured citations and relationships | DataCite, Crossref, COAR Notify, RO-Crate, CITATION.cff | Registration arrangements and schema support vary |
| Licences | Explicit machine-readable rights | SPDX identifiers and appropriate content/data licences | One licence may not suit every object |
| Principles | Responsible practice and governance | FAIR and CARE principles | Principles require context-specific implementation |
| Review and code checking | Traceable reports against defined scope | PCI, Review Commons, PREreview, CODECHECK and disciplinary communities | Examples only; public comments are not automatically formal review |

## Vendor-neutral requirement format

Each JORN requirement will publish: required outcome; minimum evidence; acceptable equivalence; discipline exceptions; privacy and accessibility conditions; recognised external assessment, if any; and an appeal or correction route. Authors will not be required to use one commercial platform.

## Relationship categories

- **Recommended service:** may help satisfy a requirement; no formal relationship.
- **Recognised assessment provider:** JORN has published conditions for accepting a specified assessment as evidence.
- **Integrated infrastructure provider:** a documented integration or metadata exchange exists.
- **Formal partner:** a signed or formally approved relationship defines responsibilities, duration, branding, data handling, conflicts, and editorial-independence safeguards.

No provider may buy preferential placement or editorial treatment. See the [relationship register](../community/relationships/).

## Directory maintenance

Every listing should show category, coverage, evidence last checked, jurisdiction or governance where relevant, cost limitations, accessibility notes, relationship status, conflicts, and a correction route. Inclusion is not a guarantee of availability, security, preservation, or fitness for every project.
