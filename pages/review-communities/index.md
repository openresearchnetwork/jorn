<div class="info-banner"><strong>No formal review-community relationships are confirmed.</strong> PCI, Review Commons, PREreview, learned societies and other communities are examples for investigation—not JORN partners or integrations.</div>

## Route 1: recognised external review

JORN checks reviewer qualifications and selection, conflicts, exact manuscript version, review criteria and scope, author response, revision history, transfer permission, transparency and procedural integrity. It may recognise the review in full, recognise only specified parts, request supplementary review, or decline recognition.

## Route 2: coordinated review

Under a documented arrangement, a specialist community may recruit field experts while JORN coordinates open-science and technical assessment. Reports, data handling, conflicts and responsibilities remain clearly attributed. JORN retains the final editorial decision.

## Route 3: JORN internal review

When no suitable community is available, the handling editor defines needed subject, methods, statistical, governance and technical expertise before recruiting reviewers. A medical-AI package, for example, may require a clinician, machine-learning or statistical-methods reviewer, privacy or data-governance specialist, and separate software evaluator.

## Recognition is evidence-specific

Recognition applies to an exact review, version and scope—not automatically to every output of an organisation. It expires or requires reassessment when criteria, ownership, process, data handling or conflicts materially change.

## Relationships must be named correctly

A recommended service is not a recognised assessment provider; a recognised provider is not necessarily integrated; and integration is not a formal partnership. All confirmed relationships must appear in the public [relationship register](../community/relationships/).

## Final responsibility

An unconflicted JORN editor decides whether review is sufficient, appoints supplementary expertise, assesses revisions and makes the accept or reject decision. External review does not guarantee JORN acceptance.
