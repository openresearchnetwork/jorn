<div class="info-banner"><strong>Status: draft guidance.</strong> JORN does not require a separate DOI for every decorative image, icon, chart panel, or minor visual element.</div>

## Minimum requirements

For applicable substantive objects, supply source data; generation code or a documented process; captions; licence and attribution; alternative text and accessibility information; provenance; version; and relationships to manuscript, data and software.

A single versioned figure-and-source-material package is often clearer than assigning an identifier to every panel. Encourage a separate DOI or persistent identifier when a visual object is independently reusable or citable, is a primary research output, or forms a substantial microscopy, mapping, audiovisual or visualisation collection.

## Review questions

- Can a reader locate the source data and exact generation process?
- Are manipulations, exclusions and composite images disclosed?
- Do captions and alternative text convey the intended information?
- Are third-party rights and attributions clear?
- Is the version used in the accepted manuscript preserved?
- Does the accessible alternative serve an equivalent purpose?

Traceability does not prove that the interpretation is correct; disciplinary reviewers remain responsible for the scientific use of the visual evidence.
