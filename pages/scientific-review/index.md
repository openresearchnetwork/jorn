## Disciplinary scientific review

Qualified field reviewers assess the research question, relevance, scholarly contribution, methodology, statistical reasoning, interpretation, relationship to existing knowledge, supported conclusions, and relevant ethical or disciplinary standards.

## Open-science implementation review

Reviewers assess repositories, identifiers, versions, licences, availability and restrictions, documentation, provenance, object connections and implementation levels. They do not convert openness into a claim of validity.

## Artifact and reproducibility assessment

Technical evaluators make bounded findings about software function, data usability, environments, workflow execution, reproduced outputs, synthetic-data utility, or secure real-data verification. A successful execution is not independent replication or a publication decision.

## Review routes

1. **Recognised external community:** JORN evaluates an existing qualified, version-specific review and may recognise all, part, or none of it.
2. **Coordinated external-community review:** a field community recruits or coordinates scientific experts while JORN coordinates complementary assessments and retains the final decision.
3. **JORN internal review:** when no suitable external community is available, a handling editor defines the expertise profile and appoints qualified reviewers.

External review reduces duplication but does not transfer JORN’s responsibility. An unconflicted JORN editor evaluates sufficiency, fills expertise gaps, oversees revision, and accepts or rejects the exact version.

## Feedback is not automatically peer review

**Open community feedback** is voluntary and not commissioned by an editor. **JORN-commissioned peer review** is performed by a qualified reviewer formally appointed by a JORN editor. **Recognised external peer review** is a formal external process accepted under published criteria. Public visibility alone does not change one category into another.
