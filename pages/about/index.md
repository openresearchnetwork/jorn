## Why JORN?

Research is increasingly distributed. A manuscript may be on arXiv, data in Zenodo, software on GitHub and Software Heritage, a protocol in a specialist repository, and contributor identities in ORCID. Yet the formal publication often reduces that connected work to a PDF and a supplement link.

JORN is designed around a different premise: **the journal should be the trusted editorial and certification layer, not the owner of every research object.**

<blockquote class="statement-quote">
<p>Open research, reviewed by an open network.</p>
<cite>JORN’s founding vision</cite>
</blockquote>

## What the journal will do

JORN will be directly accountable for the scholarly functions that cannot safely be delegated:

<div class="responsibility-grid" markdown>
<div markdown>### Select & review
Define scope, appoint qualified editors, coordinate independent review, and make decisions.</div>
<div markdown>### Connect & certify
Identify exact versions, link research objects, and publish authoritative metadata.</div>
<div markdown>### Protect integrity
Manage conflicts, ethics, corrections, retractions, complaints, and appeals.</div>
<div markdown>### Steward the record
Maintain discoverability, preservation planning, continuity, and public accountability.</div>
</div>

## Our place in the network

- **Open Research Network** is the wider community and open-research initiative.
- **JORN** is its proposed peer-review, publication, and scholarly-certification program.
- **Open Science Labs** supports the initiative and its development.

The relationship is intended to bring researchers, independent scholars, open-source contributors, research software engineers, data communities, and reproducibility initiatives into a shared publication program.

## Current status

JORN is in a concept and founding phase. Its name, exact scope, governance, platform, legal responsibilities, and editorial policies require formal due diligence and community agreement before launch. This website presents the direction of travel transparently; it does not claim that the journal is already accepting or certifying research.
