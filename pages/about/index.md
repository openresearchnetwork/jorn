<div class="info-banner"><strong>Current status:</strong> JORN is in a founding and feasibility phase. It is not accepting formal submissions and has not published or certified any research.</div>

## The problem JORN addresses

Computational research rarely exists as one document. A manuscript may be in a preprint repository, data in a specialist archive, software in a source repository and preservation service, and protocols or environments elsewhere. A reader can struggle to determine which versions were actually reviewed and how they relate to the editorial decision.

JORN is designed to add an accountable scholarly-publication layer without taking over the work of suitable repositories.

<blockquote class="statement-quote">
<p>JORN does not replace research repositories.</p>
<cite>It provides the editorial, publication, and stewardship layer that turns distributed research objects into one citable and accountable journal record.</cite>
</blockquote>

## What makes JORN a journal

JORN will qualify as a journal because it will receive eligible submissions, appoint qualified editors, coordinate independent peer review, request and evaluate revisions, make formal acceptance or rejection decisions, publish accepted work under the JORN title, register publication metadata, and maintain corrections, retractions, appeals, and continuing status.

Technical checks or a certification seal alone do not constitute journal publication.

## What makes the record distinctive

After acceptance, JORN will publish a permanent **JORN Publication Record** containing the citation and DOI, abstract, exact accepted manuscript, data and software versions, computational environment, declarations, peer-review reports, author responses, editorial decision, certification profile, optional badge evidence, limitations, and current status.

The JORN DOI will identify this journal record. Repository identifiers will continue to identify their underlying research objects. Structured metadata will connect them.

## What JORN certifies

The universal **JORN Certified Record** seal will state that JORN peer-reviewed, accepted, published, and connected the exact versions identified in the Publication Record. It applies only to the assessment scope described there.

Certification does not guarantee permanent correctness, complete error detection, open access to all data, computational reproduction of every result, independent replication, or immunity from future software and platform changes.

JORN is therefore different from a standalone reproducibility-checking service. Computational checking may contribute optional evidence, but acceptance requires formal scientific and editorial judgment.

## Institutional relationships

| Participant | Role during the founding phase |
| --- | --- |
| Open Research Network | The broader collaboration and community initiative |
| JORN | The proposed journal and formal scholarly-publication program |
| Open Science Labs | A supporting organisation helping develop the initiative |
| Legal publisher | **[TO BE CONFIRMED BEFORE LAUNCH]** |
| Editorial leadership | Must hold independent authority over journal decisions; appointments are **[TO BE CONFIRMED]** |
| Infrastructure providers | May supply tools or services but will not control editorial outcomes |
| Funders and partners | May support operations but will receive no editorial privilege |

JORN is **being designed** as a community-governed journal. It should not be described as community-governed in operation until governance bodies, appointments, decision rights, terms, voting arrangements, and accountability mechanisms exist.

## Present status

The title, legal publisher, platform, DOI and preservation arrangements, editorial appointments, pilot dates, final policies, partner relationships, funding route, and submission process require confirmation. The website labels proposed features explicitly so that a design intention is not mistaken for an operational service.
