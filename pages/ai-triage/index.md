## Why use assisted screening

A research package can span many repositories, identifiers and versions. An approved system may help editors find missing objects, inconsistent links and documentation gaps, then draft a remediation and expertise-routing report. It must expose its evidence and uncertainty rather than predict impact or acceptance.

> AI checks whether the research package appears ready for responsible human evaluation. An external field community reviews it when available; otherwise, JORN recruits qualified reviewers. A JORN editor remains accountable for the final decision.

## Three screening outcomes

### Ready for human review

The public or authorised package appears sufficiently complete for an editor to consider review routing. An editor must confirm this outcome. It does not mean the research is correct, ethical, publishable, certified, or likely to be accepted.

### Author action required

Specific missing, inaccessible, inconsistent, or insufficiently documented elements should be addressed. During the pilot, this is a request for remediation—not an autonomous rejection. Authors can correct facts, explain an exception, or request human screening.

### Human editorial triage required

Uncertainty, sensitive content, unusual methods, restricted access, ethics or governance questions, conflicting evidence, or poor model coverage requires direct assessment. Sensitive flags are not published automatically.

## Permitted checks

AI may assist with scope classification; completeness; links among objects; DOI and identifier extraction; licence and version detection; data, software and synthetic-data statements; internal consistency; claim–evidence mapping; reporting-guideline prompts; reviewer-expertise profiles; external-community suggestions; possible conflicts for human investigation; and a remediation report.

Deterministic tools are preferred for DOI resolution, checksum comparison, file validation, link checking, licence detection and metadata-schema validation. Generative models should be limited to interpretation, explanation, classification and draft synthesis.

## Prohibited judgments

AI must not autonomously decide correctness, importance, novelty, misconduct, ethical adequacy, medical-model safety, reviewer appointment, acceptance, certification, corrections, expressions of concern or retraction. Screening must not use protected characteristics, prestige, institutional reputation, geography, citation counts or predicted impact as proxies for merit.

## Human oversight and author rights

During the pilot, a human editor confirms every report and every recommendation to proceed. Human–AI disagreement is recorded. Authors may inspect the report, correct factual errors, explain missing elements, request human reconsideration, and use an appeal route separate from the original operator **[TO BE CONFIRMED]**. Formal reviewer appointment always remains human-controlled.

## AI-Assisted Initial Screening Report

The private report should record system and model version, date, criteria version, exact manuscript and artifact versions, materials supplied, scope, completeness, gaps, ethical or governance flags, uncertainty, suggested expertise and communities, routing outcome, human confirmation, author corrections, and audit history.

Confirmed factual elements may later be disclosed in the Open Research Record. Unverified integrity, privacy or ethics concerns must not be published automatically.

## Confidentiality and security

Public preprints and artifacts may be analysed under a disclosed process. Do not upload unpublished manuscripts, confidential reviews, reviewer identities, private correspondence, controlled patient data, personal data, ethics documentation, security-sensitive code or proprietary material to consumer or unapproved AI systems.

Sensitive processing requires an approved secure environment, no provider training on submitted content, retention and deletion rules, access control, logging, encryption, lawful processing, contracts, and privacy and security review.

## Pilot evaluation

JORN should publish aggregate performance by discipline and package type: false-ready and false-action rates, missed items, unsupported flags, human overrides, author corrections, accessibility, cost and time. Launch thresholds, audit method, incident route and model-change controls are **[TO BE CONFIRMED BEFORE OPERATION]**.
