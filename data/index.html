Home/Data and responsible access

Prepare

As open as possible; as restricted as necessary.

JORN evaluates whether data are traceable, governed, documented, and responsibly reviewable—not whether every record is public.

Status: draft guidance. JORN has not confirmed a secure-data capability. Do not send restricted or personal data until an approved route and privacy notice are published.

Accepted access categories¶

JORN’s proposed framework recognises open real data, controlled-access real data, secure-environment access, embargoed data, synthetic companion or evaluation data, simulation-generated data, public metadata with restricted records, data not publicly shareable with a reviewed justification, and not applicable.

Restrictions do not automatically reduce the implementation level. The record must describe why access is limited, the governing authority, public metadata, request or verification route, eligible users, conditions, version, retention or embargo, and what JORN could and could not inspect.

Minimum evidence¶

  • Persistent identifier and exact version, or a documented reason one cannot be issued
  • Data dictionary, provenance, collection or generation methods, and quality limitations
  • Licence for open data or clear terms and governance for restricted data
  • Ethics, consent, community or Indigenous governance and lawful-access documentation where applicable
  • Relationship to manuscript, software, figures, protocols and derived objects
  • Responsible preservation and change-control route
  • Citation instructions

Responsible verification¶

A public metadata record plus a synthetic companion dataset can support software testing but does not establish that the reported analysis matches restricted real data. A separate secure verification may be required. The Open Research Record must distinguish materials accessible to readers from materials available only to an authorised evaluator.

What JORN will not claim¶

Data availability does not prove validity. Restriction does not prove weak practice. A synthetic dataset is not automatically anonymous. An evaluator’s access does not entitle JORN to redistribute the data. Every verified claim must stay within the exact evidence and access scope.
