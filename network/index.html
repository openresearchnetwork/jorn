Participation routes

Build the expertise a responsible journal requires.

JORN’s network is being formed. Participation can inform or support the journal, but it does not automatically confer editorial authority.

Network status: forming. Roles, selection, workload, recognition, payment, conflicts, and contact routes remain to be confirmed. No organisation is a formal partner unless JORN publishes that relationship.

Institutional roles¶

  • Open Research Network is the broader collaboration and community initiative.
  • JORN is the proposed journal and formal scholarly-publication program.
  • Open Science Labs supports development of the initiative.
  • Editorial leadership must hold independent authority over journal decisions.
  • Funders, partners, and infrastructure providers may support operations but receive no editorial privilege.

Selection and conflicts¶

Editorial and assessment roles should be filled according to documented expertise, independence, conduct expectations, availability, and conflicts. Community membership, financial support, or institutional status must not guarantee appointment.

For each assignment, JORN should consider recent collaboration, supervisory relationships, institutional dependence, financial interests, competition, advocacy roles, and other circumstances that could affect—or reasonably appear to affect—judgment.

Workload, recognition, and payment¶

Before recruitment, each role should have a realistic task description, estimated time, training and support, confidentiality terms, recognition route, and a clear statement of whether work is voluntary, paid, or institutionally supported.

JORN should not build its sustainability model around unlimited unpaid labour. Managing-editor work, intensive artifact and computational evaluation, integrity cases, accessibility, privacy, metadata, and coordination require funded capacity.

Standards and initiatives used as benchmarks¶

JORN can learn from Episciences; Peer Community In and Peer Community Journal; ACM Artifact Review and Badging; CODECHECK; Center for Open Science badges and TOP Guidelines; COPE; DOAJ; DataCite; Crossref; COAR Notify; ORCID; CRediT; and Software Heritage.

These are reference points, not claimed partners. Any future partnership will be named only after agreement and disclosure of its purpose and safeguards.

Review and infrastructure ecosystem¶

The proposed network includes external field-review communities; handling editors; JORN-commissioned scientific reviewers; open-science reviewers; artifact evaluators and codecheckers; synthetic-data, privacy and clinical specialists; repositories and libraries; software-review organisations; and metadata, accessibility and preservation experts. Roles remain distinct, appointed authority is explicit, and participation does not imply partnership.

Suggested communities and services will be evaluated under published criteria. Until a relationship appears in the public register, it is only a recommendation or candidate for investigation.

JORN uses five relationship labels consistently: external review community, recommended service, recognised assessment provider, integrated infrastructure provider, and formal partner. One label does not imply another. No provider is currently confirmed as integrated or formally partnered; recognised providers must be listed with their qualifying assessment scope and evidence.
