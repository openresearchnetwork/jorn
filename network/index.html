Participate

A journal is only as strong as its community.

JORN brings together researchers, reviewers, editors, infrastructure providers, and open-science communities to build a trustworthy publication program.

The relationship¶

The Open Research Network is the broader community and collaboration initiative. JORN is its proposed peer-review and scholarly-certification program. Open Science Labs supports the development of both.

This structure is intended to welcome contributors who often sit across institutional boundaries: independent researchers, open-source maintainers, research software engineers, data scientists, librarians, reproducibility initiatives, and disciplinary communities.

Partner principles¶

Partnership does not confer editorial privilege. Any formal relationship should be transparent, mission-aligned, and governed by written safeguards for editorial independence. JORN should prefer partners that support open standards, portable data, persistent identifiers, and community accountability.

Potential connections may include open repositories, scholarly identifier services, preservation networks, universities, libraries, research-software communities, and independent research organizations. Names will only be presented as partners after a relationship is confirmed.

Founding roadmap¶

01FeasibilityMission, scope, publisher, platform, legal needs, and founding group
02Policy & governanceEditors, peer review, integrity, conflicts, data, software, and ethics
03Technical prototypeSubmission, metadata, repositories, DOI workflow, review, and preservation
04Pilot collectionFive to ten independently reviewed submissions and workflow evaluation
05Public launchOpen submissions, first collection, identifiers, discovery, and reporting

Follow the work¶

JORN’s development will take place openly wherever privacy and editorial integrity permit. Public project links and participation forms will be added as the founding governance is confirmed.

Build the network with us¶

Share the project with a community that cares about open, rigorous, and reproducible research—or support the infrastructure and coordination required to launch it responsibly.
