Draft framework

Policies must be approved, versioned, and accountable.

JORN has not adopted operational editorial policies. This register identifies the documents required before pilot review and how their status will be reported.

Register status: planning. The entries below are requirements, not short-form policies. None is operational unless a published record shows an approving body, effective date, version, and revision history.

Policy status model¶

Every policy page will display:

  • Status: draft, public consultation, approved, or operational
  • Responsible body: author, reviewer, approver, and operational owner
  • Version and effective date: including the rules applicable to each submission
  • History: consultation, changes, prior versions, and reasons for revision
  • Contact and escalation: questions, appeals, complaints, and urgent concerns

Current policy direction¶

Diamond Open Access¶

JORN is designed to charge neither authors nor readers. Authors should retain copyright, with CC BY 4.0 as the preferred article-content license. These commitments require confirmation by the legal publisher and a sustainable funding model.

Open peer review¶

The proposed default is to publish reports, author responses, and decisions after acceptance. Reviewers may sign by consent or remain confidential. Restricted disclosure is allowed where openness creates credible risk. Terms must be known before participation.

Responsible data access¶

JORN will not equate public data with responsible data. A justified controlled-access, embargoed, synthetic, or non-shareable arrangement can be eligible if the journal can evaluate it lawfully and securely.

Certification and scoring¶

Certification will be bounded to exact versions and the assessment described. JORN will not publish an aggregate score, trust percentage, grade, star rating, or publication level. Optional evidence badges remain separate from the universal Certified Record seal.

Accessibility¶

JORN intends to use WCAG 2.2 AA as a design and testing target. It should not claim conformance until a documented audit has been completed. The final policy must define testing, ownership, alternative formats, reporting, remediation times, and known exceptions.

Before any policy becomes operational¶

The legal publisher, responsible governance body, editor authority, privacy contact, complaint route, and public version repository must be confirmed. Approved policies should remain accessible after revision so readers can determine which version governed a particular Publication Record.
