Home/Recognition of external assessments

Review and certification

Reuse qualified evidence without outsourcing responsibility.

JORN may recognise specified external scientific, software, data, artifact or reproducibility assessments when their exact scope and process meet published criteria.

Status: framework only. No assessment provider is currently recognised. Examples elsewhere on this site are candidates for investigation, not confirmed relationships.

Recognition criteria¶

JORN checks the provider’s governance and independence; assessor qualifications and appointment; conflicts; review criteria; exact object and version; evidence and report; author response; revision history; confidentiality and transfer permission; transparency; complaints and corrections; and continuing availability.

Recognition decision¶

A JORN editor or delegated qualified evaluator records whether evidence is recognised in full, in part, requires supplementary assessment, or is declined. The decision identifies the accepted claim and exclusions. Provider reputation alone is insufficient.

Continuing controls¶

Recognition is versioned, periodically reviewed and withdrawable. Material changes to criteria, ownership, funding, data handling, security or assessor selection trigger reassessment. Paid inclusion or preferential editorial treatment is prohibited.

Provider record¶

Every recognised provider listing should publish relationship category, qualifying assessment types, applicable criteria version, start and review dates, evidence, conflicts, data flows, limitations, status and decision owner.
