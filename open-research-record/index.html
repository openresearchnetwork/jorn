About

One permanent journal record for distributed research.

After formal acceptance, JORN will publish an accessible, citable page connecting the exact manuscript, research objects, reviews, decisions, certification evidence, restrictions and status history.

Status: proposed publication format. JORN has not issued a DOI or published an accepted record. Registration, platform and preservation arrangements are **[TO BE CONFIRMED BEFORE PUBLICATION]**.

Publication, certification, and underlying objects¶

A JORN Open Research Record is the permanent journal publication produced only after an accountable JORN editor accepts the exact research package. A JORN Certified Open Research Record is the certification status attached after JORN reviewed, accepted, published and connected the exact objects and evidence named in that record. Underlying manuscripts, data, software, models, protocols, figures and reviews remain preserved in suitable repositories or platforms.

A readiness report, AI screening, external software review, technical check, badge or repository deposit alone is not a JORN publication.

DOI and version model¶

The JORN DOI identifies the Open Research Record. Underlying objects keep their own identifiers: manuscript or preprint DOI, data and synthetic-data DOI, software DOI or SWHID, protocol or preregistration identifier, figure-package DOI, model identifier and review identifier. Accessible HTML and PDF render the same publication and do not receive competing DOIs.

Acceptance applies to exact versions. A material later change requires a visible correction, update, superseding record, expression of concern, withdrawal or retraction—not silent replacement.

Required record sections¶

  1. Bibliographic information: title, authors, affiliations, ORCIDs, RORs, CRediT roles, corresponding author, type, JORN DOI, dates, licence, version and status
  2. Scholarly abstract and plain-language summary
  3. Editorial summary of relevance, route, expertise, strengths, limitations and decision basis
  4. AI-triage statement, including system, purpose, inputs, human oversight, corrections and decision boundary
  5. Dimensional Open Science Implementation Profile
  6. Connected object table and relationship map
  7. Data, synthetic-data, software, figures and media statements
  8. Scientific reviews, author responses, revisions, handling editor and final decision
  9. Open-science, artifact and reproducibility reports
  10. Certification, badges, verified claims, unassessed matters and limitations
  11. Ethics, privacy, consent and community governance
  12. External services and assessments, with relationship category
  13. Corrections and status history
  14. Complete-record and artifact-level citations

Connected object table¶

Field Required content
Object Type and title
Identity Persistent identifier and exact version
Location Host and preservation route
Rights and access Licence, public, controlled, embargoed or other terms
Relationship Is supplement to, is source of, is reviewed version of, or another explicit relation
Assessment State, assessor, criteria, date, evidence and external review
Limits Exclusions, restrictions, unavailable elements and change risk

Accessible and machine-readable publication¶

The canonical format should be accessible HTML with a consistent PDF rendering, printable summary, BibTeX, RIS, CSL JSON, EndNote XML and plain-text citations. JSON-LD and RO-Crate or an equivalent structured export should represent people, organisations, versions, licences, roles, reviews, decisions, claims and object relationships.

Continuing stewardship¶

JORN remains responsible for its DOI, metadata, editorial evidence, certificate status, notices and closure plan even when repositories hold the underlying files. Link monitoring, checksums where appropriate, preservation metadata and visible status changes protect the meaning of the original decision.
