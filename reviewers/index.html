Review and certification

Recruit expertise for the gaps external review cannot cover.

JORN will appoint qualified reviewers directly when no suitable external community is available or when an external review leaves a defined expertise gap.

Status: network being designed. Joining an interest list will not itself make anyone a JORN reviewer or confer editorial authority.

Appointment begins with an expertise profile¶

Before recruitment, the handling editor records the subject knowledge, methods, statistics, community context, data governance and technical expertise needed. Selection should match that profile rather than institutional prestige, seniority alone or public volunteering.

Recruitment routes¶

JORN may use direct invitations, carefully moderated open calls, referrals from qualified communities, and the reviewer-interest register. An editor verifies identity, relevant work, current competence, availability and conflicts before formal appointment. Public comments may inform review but do not become commissioned peer review automatically.

Safeguards¶

Reviewers disclose financial, professional, personal, supervisory, competitive and advocacy conflicts. The editor documents eligibility and recusal. Confidential materials stay within approved systems. Harassment, coercion, discriminatory review and attempts to identify confidential participants are subject to moderation and complaint procedures.

Identity and publication¶

The proposed default is to publish reports, author responses and editorial decisions after acceptance. Reviewers may sign by informed consent or remain confidential. Any disclosure exception and reason should be recorded. Rejected or withdrawn reviews require separate policy and consent.

Credit, workload and compensation¶

JORN should offer citable reports where appropriate, ORCID-compatible credit, contribution acknowledgement and clear deadlines. Workload must be monitored; repeated unpaid labour must not be assumed. Honoraria or other compensation may be appropriate for intensive scientific, software, privacy, clinical or secure-data evaluation. Rates, eligibility, funding and tax treatment are [TO BE CONFIRMED] and cannot affect editorial outcomes.
