Home/Aims & scope

Founding scope

Independent publication of reproducible computational research.

JORN will evaluate methodological validity, reporting, integrity, traceability, and scholarly contribution—not predicted popularity or broad claims of novelty.

Proposed founding scope. Formal submissions are not open, and the editorial board must approve detailed eligibility criteria before the pilot begins.

Journal aim¶

JORN aims to publish citable, accountable journal records for research that is distributed across manuscripts, data, software, environments, protocols, and repositories. Formal editorial assessment and independent peer review will determine whether work is accepted under the JORN title.

The journal is designed to make the exact evidence and assessment scope traceable. Open availability is valuable, but openness alone does not establish validity or trust.

Founding pilot scope¶

The pilot will focus narrowly on independent publication and certification of reproducible computational research. JORN should accept only work for which it can appoint qualified editors, reviewers, and—where applicable—artifact or computational evaluators.

Initial article types¶

01Reproducible computational research articlesResearch in which code, data, environments, and computational workflows are central to the reported findings.
02Research software and computational methods papersSoftware or methods with a clear research contribution, documentation, tests, an archived release, and an identifiable reuse case.
03Reproducibility and replication reportsIndependent attempts to reproduce computational results or replicate prior findings, including informative unsuccessful attempts.

Acceptance will not depend on receiving an optional evidence badge. Scientific peer review, artifact evaluation, and computational reproduction checks are related but distinct assessments.

Common eligibility requirements¶

Every submission should provide:

  • a clear research contribution or well-defined reproduction or replication question;
  • methods and computational reasoning reported in sufficient detail for review;
  • an exact, persistently identified manuscript version;
  • an archived software release, source repository, tests, and environment information where software is relevant;
  • exact data identifiers and versions, or a documented restriction and reviewable access route;
  • relevant protocols, preregistrations, funding, ethics, and competing-interest declarations;
  • authorship, ORCID identifiers, and CRediT contribution roles; and
  • licenses or access terms appropriate to each research object.

Responsible restrictions¶

Public access is not always ethical or lawful. Controlled access, an embargo, synthetic data, or non-sharing may be appropriate for privacy, consent, security, Indigenous or community governance, endangered species, confidential interviews, or protected health information.

JORN will evaluate whether the restriction is justified and whether responsible review is possible. It will report the access category neutrally rather than treat restricted data as a lower score.

Possible future scope¶

Not accepted during the founding pilot: general dataset papers, non-computational methods papers, research-infrastructure reports, registered reports, broad multidisciplinary research articles, community-led research across all methodologies, and qualitative research requiring a distinct evaluation framework.

These areas may be considered only after the journal has appropriate editorial expertise, policies, governance, evaluation criteria, and sustainable capacity. Listing them here is not a commitment to accept them later.

Out of scope¶

The pilot should decline work that falls outside the three article types; lacks a traceable manuscript, software release, environment, or evidence route; cannot be reviewed responsibly; has unresolved ethics or integrity concerns; or requires expertise and security JORN cannot provide.

JORN is not a repository, preprint server, badge vendor, software-testing service, or venue for technical certification without formal journal review and an editorial decision.

Long-term mission and controlled expansion¶

JORN’s long-term mission is to support connected and certified open research records across disciplines. Expansion beyond the founding computational scope requires demonstrated editorial expertise, discipline-specific implementation guidance, appropriate review communities or internal reviewers, ethics and governance capacity, sustainable funding, and approved policies. Long-term ambition must not be presented as a current service.
