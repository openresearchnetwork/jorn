The journal

Open research as a connected practice.

JORN is being designed for rigorous contributions whose value depends on accessible evidence, reusable methods, and transparent scholarly exchange.

Our aim¶

The Journal of the Open Research Network aims to make open research easier to evaluate, trust, reuse, and extend. It will provide scholarly certification for work already deposited in open repositories and connect the parts of research that conventional article pages often leave scattered.

JORN will judge work on methodological quality, clarity, integrity, reproducibility, and the value of its contribution—not on predicted popularity or narrow claims of novelty.

Founding subject areas¶

01Reproducible computational researchAnalyses with accessible code, environments, and evidence.
02Research softwareSoftware with a clear scholarly contribution, documentation, tests, and an archived release.
03Data-intensive researchResearch in which data quality, provenance, and reuse are central.
04Methods and protocolsOpen methods that enable reliable and transferable research practice.
05Replication and meta-researchStudies that test, synthesize, or improve how knowledge is produced.
06Open research infrastructureTools, standards, and community systems supporting open scholarship.

Publication types¶

The initial portfolio may include research articles, software papers, dataset papers, reproducibility reports, replication studies, methods papers, registered reports, research notes, and open-infrastructure reports. The founding editorial board will establish precise criteria before submissions open.

What every submission should demonstrate¶

  • A clear research question, contribution, or reuse case
  • Appropriate and transparently reported methods
  • Public access to the manuscript and all shareable supporting objects
  • Documentation sufficient for informed evaluation and reuse
  • Persistent identifiers and version information
  • Ethical and legal handling of people, animals, communities, and sensitive data
  • Complete authorship, funding, and competing-interest disclosures

Out of scope¶

JORN will not certify work simply because it is publicly available. Incomplete submissions, unverifiable claims, inappropriate repository deposits, missing ethical approval, undisclosed conflicts, or inaccessible central evidence may be declined during screening.

During the pilot, the journal may also decline sound work when the editorial network cannot provide the specialist expertise required for responsible review.
