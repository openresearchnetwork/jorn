A journal for open research

Keep the research where it belongs.
Connect it where it matters.

Research does not live in a single PDF. It spans repositories, codebases, datasets, protocols, and conversations. JORN provides the editorial layer that reviews, connects, and certifies these distributed objects—without rebuilding the infrastructure that already serves researchers well.

Why JORN →
01

Distributed by design

Manuscripts, data, and software remain in trusted repositories built to host and preserve them.

02

Certified with care

Editors and independent reviewers assess the research and identify the exact version accepted.

03

Transparent by default

Versions, reviews, responses, decisions, and corrections form a visible editorial history.

One scholarly record

Everything needed to understand, verify, and build on the work.

Every JORN publication will be a persistent certification record: the authoritative map between an accepted manuscript, the evidence behind it, and the people and process that evaluated it.

  • Exact reviewed and accepted versions
  • Linked data, software, protocols, and preregistrations
  • Editorial decisions, reviews, and author responses
  • Machine-readable metadata and persistent identifiers

How publishing works

Rigorous review.
A lighter footprint.

Authors begin by sharing their work in an appropriate open repository. JORN focuses on the work a journal uniquely must do: editorial assessment, peer review, certification, and stewardship.

Full editorial process →
1

Deposit

Share the manuscript and research objects in trusted open repositories.

2

Connect

Provide persistent links for data, software, protocols, identities, and funding.

3

Review

Editors and independent peers assess validity, transparency, and reproducibility.

4

Certify

JORN identifies the accepted version and publishes the complete editorial record.
