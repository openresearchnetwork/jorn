Why JORN is a journal

Formal editorial judgment—not a badge attached to a repository deposit.

JORN will receive eligible submissions, appoint qualified editors, coordinate independent peer review, assess revisions, make an acceptance or rejection decision, and publish accepted work under the JORN title. It will then maintain corrections, appeals, retractions, metadata, and continuing status.

About JORN →
01

Independent journal review

Scientific peers assess methods, analysis, reporting, integrity, traceability, contribution, and limitations. An unconflicted editor makes the formal decision.

02

Permanent Publication Record

The citable JORN article page identifies exact versions, links research objects, publishes editorial evidence, and states current status.

03

Bounded certification

The universal seal confirms a formal process for the listed versions and assessment scope. It is not a guarantee of permanent correctness.

The primary publication

One accountable record for the work JORN accepted.

The manuscript, data, software, protocols, and environments may stay in appropriate repositories. JORN publishes the authoritative journal page, editorial history, certification evidence, limitations, status, metadata, and citation.

  • JORN DOI for the journal Publication Record
  • Repository identifiers for underlying research objects
  • Exact versions and structured metadata relationships
  • Visible corrections, supersession, withdrawal, or retraction

How publication will work

Deposit. Review. Decide. Publish.

Repository deposit starts the process; it does not equal publication. Optional artifact and computational assessments may add evidence, but only formal editorial acceptance leads to a JORN Publication Record.

Full proposed process →
1

Deposit & connect

Identify the exact manuscript, data, software, environment, protocols, declarations, and access terms.

2

Review & assess

Scientific peers review the work. Separate artifact or computational evaluation occurs only where applicable.

3

Revise & decide

Authors update repository versions and respond. An unconflicted editor accepts, rejects, or requests revision.

4

Publish & steward

JORN publishes its citable record, certification profile, evidence, limits, metadata, and continuing status.

Narrow founding scope

Certification and evidence

A clear profile instead of a misleading score.

Universal

JORN Certified Record

Confirms review, acceptance, publication, and connection of the exact identified versions.

Optional badge

Artifacts Evaluated—Functional

Reports that specified artifacts were documented, accessible to the evaluator, and functional for the assessed purpose.

Optional badge

Computational Results Reproduced

Reports that specified primary outputs were obtained within stated comparison criteria or tolerance.

No ranking

No aggregate score

Validity, ethics, access, documentation, function, and reproduction remain separate, visible dimensions.
